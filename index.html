Pre Colonial Era

In the pre-colonial Philippines, villages were governed by a set of laws that
included civil and criminal provisions. Usually, it was the datu and the
village that promulgated such rules, which were then to the people by a town
crier called the umalohokan.The Philippines had a very advanced civilization
long before the arrival of the Spaniards. When the colonizers arrived,
everything that contradicted their own system had to be removed.
Sculptures, texts, religious ceremonies, and virtually anything else deemed obscene, evil or a threat to their rule were eliminated.

 

In terms of food, our forefathers did not suffer from any lack thereof. Blessed with such a resource-rich country, they had enough for themselves and their families. Forests, rivers, and seas yielded plentiful supplies of meat, fish, and other foodstuffs.

 

Polygamists in pre-colonial India were allowed to divorce, and women could choose how many children they wanted. Sexuality was not as suppressed, and no premium was given to virginity before marriage. Homosexuals were also largely tolerated, seeing as how some of the babaylans were actually men in drag.

Spanish Era

During Spanish colonialism of the Philippines, the government was divided into two branches: executive and judiciary. There was no legislative branch on that time since the laws of the islands were coming from Spain . In the Philippines, the only laws enacted are those mandated by the Governor General. The Governor General led the government at the time. He was regarded as the King's and Spain's representative. He is the highest ranking officer on the island and is in charge of enforcing mother country laws.

 

He also has a power to appoint or relieve officer in the parish, except those appointed by the king of Spain. "Alcalde Mayor" had both the executive and judicial power. In 1886, their executive power was abolished but their judicial powers remain. Filipino American National Historical Society declared October Filipino American History Month to preserve and promote Filipino-American history. Ocampo noted that when Filipinos immigrated to the U.S., many would end up at churches and meet Mexican Americans rather than other Asian Americans.

Japanese Era

The 1943 Constitution was the constitution of the Japanese-sponsored Second Republic of the Philippines (1943-1945). It was recognized as legitimate and binding only in Japanese-controlled areas but was ignored by the United States government and the Philippine Commonwealth government in-exile. In June 1943, the Preparatory Commission for Philippine Independence (PCPI) was created to draft a new constitution. The Japanese monitored the drafting of the constitution and instructed the PCPI that the constitution should conform with the principles of the Greater East Asia Co-Prosperity Sphere.

 

The invasion of the Philippines started on December 8, 1941, ten hours after the attack on Pearl Harbor. Lacking air cover, the American Asiatic Fleet in the Philippines withdrew to Java. Douglas MacArthur escaped Corregidor on the night of March 11, 1942 for Australia, 4,000 km away.

 

The President is the head of government and commander-in-chief of the Armed Forces. The Assembly is composed of representatives from each province elected in Kalibapi conventions throughout the country. The judicial power is exercised by the Supreme Court whose justices, together with judges of lower courts, are to be appointed by the President.
